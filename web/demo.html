Explain like I'm 5 why this matters

Imagine a robot shopkeeper. It has a piggy bank full of digital dollars (USDC). When you ask it a question about a company, it:

  • 1. Tells you the price first.
  • 2. You drop coins in its piggy bank.
  • 3. It pays another robot (a researcher) to do the work.
  • 4. It gives you the answer plus a receipt showing every coin in and out.
  • 5. If a job costs too much, it raises its price all by itself.

No human approves the trades. The piggy bank itself is the rule book.

Concepts a tiny glossary

  • x402 — an HTTP standard: server replies 402 Payment Required with a price & address. Client pays, retries, gets the answer.
  • USDC — a stablecoin: 1 USDC ≈ $1. We use it on Circle's Arc Testnet so demo money isn't real money.
  • Circle Agent Wallet — programmable wallet held by the agent. Becomes the company's treasury.
  • Gateway / batched settlement — Circle batches multiple x402 payments into one settlement; receipts may say pending-batch briefly.
  • CFO loop — Claude Agent SDK decides: pay supplier? deny on budget? reprice? Each decision is logged with reasoning.
  • Receipt — the proof: revenue, wholesale, margin, both on-chain tx hashes.

Talking points
  • Two-sided commerce — money in & out, not one-shot.
  • Reprice on loss — agent raises price after a thin margin.
  • Decline-before-charge — never collect 402 you can't fulfil.
